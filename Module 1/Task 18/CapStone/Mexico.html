Mexico: La Vida Loca:

Mexico has a special place in my heart. The food, people and atomsphere. I'm conversational in Spanish and it really let me meet tons of great people that way.

I lived in Ciudad de Mexico (DF), the largest city in the Western Hemisphere. It's a massive maze of history, music and street food. DF has more museums than any city in the world. It's very hard to get bored when you have Luche Libre fights, football events, music, bars, markets and festivals happening all around you.

The weather is prefect in DF, an eternal spring all year round, and quick flights to beach towns across the country provide an amazing escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. If beaches aren't your thing Mexico has great mountainous areas, and desert in the north.

Mexico has a lot of history to it as well. It's museums and well preserved heritage sites are amazing to visit and show the rich history from early Aztec time, Spanish Conquistors and Modern Mexico. My wife, Nikky, lived in Coyoacan, home to Freda Kahlo and Diego Riveria. We enjoyed spending our weekend there as it was almost like a town inside DF.

Some Amazing things About Mexico

Hard to name them all but here are a few of the highlights about living in Mexico.

THE FOOD

Food is everywhere, on every street corner people are selling amazing food. Guys on bicycles with baskets carrying "Tacos de Cansatas" (Basket Tacos). Fresh produce is abound, fresh juice stalls litter the streets and great chocolate. From Mole to Pozole and of course the best tacos in the world! There are so many great foods to eat in Mexico

Markets

Markets are a big part of Mexican life, and unlike Chinese markets, they're clean, briming with fresh produce. DF also has the second largest sea food market in the world although landlocked. Markets in Mexico aren't always about food, there are tons of markets usually there is some theme involved in them as well. Perfect for going out and browsing in the afternoon.

Fiestas

Mexico knows how to party, great bars and restuarants are a plenty in DF. Parties are just part of family culture. I used to play football (soccer) every Sunday and win or loose we'd go back to my friends house with his whole family and neighbors and have a party. Throuhgout the year there are many fiestivals, like Day of the Dead. Music is everywhere, there used to be a guy who played the trumpet up and down my street.

The Down Side to Mexico

Although I adore Mexico there is a side to it that I'm not a fan of.

Violence

It's no secret that the Cartels run Mexico. The newspapers are littered everyday of grusome murders, bodies of people who crossed the cartel on full display for the public to see. Luckily I never experience any of that part of Mexico.

Crowded Public Transport

I lived in Asia for years so I thought I knew about crowded public transport. Mexico City is different, the metro is run down and old and although the bus system is new it's packed. You squeeze in take your last breath of fresh air and hope you get to your stop as soon as possible. Mexico City is polluted as well so all the smog from a city based in a valley hovers down on you some days.

Montezuma's Revenge

A foreigner, and probably a local, should never drink the water in Mexico. Don't forget ice is also water! There were many times my stomach would drop and I'd have to race to the bathroom. The unforunate part of living in Mexico with such great food is that you'll spend a lot of time on the toilet as well.
